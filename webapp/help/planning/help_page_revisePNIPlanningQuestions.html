Revise PNI planning questions

Your answers to the PNI planning questions constitute a sort of agreement, with your team or just with yourself, on what your project is about (and what it is not about). You can use these answers as a touchstone to remind yourself what you are doing and why. You can also use them to communicate the intent behind the project with people outside it.

What to do here

You can copy your draft answers (entered on the Answer PNI planning questions page) and revise them in the light of the planning activities you did:

  • Describe participant groups
  • Describe your resources
  • Tell project stories
  • Create project story elements
  • Assess story sharing

These will be your final answers, so make sure you are happy with them before you move on to the next phase of your project.

Connections to other pages

The information you enter here will appear in the project report.

Frequently-asked questions

If I make a change to this page, will it be copied to the previous Answer PNI planning questions page?

No. The flow of information goes only one way, from your draft answers to your final answers. Any changes you make on this page will not appear on the earlier page.
