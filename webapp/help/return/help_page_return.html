Return

In the return phase of a participatory narrative inquiry project, what has been gathered and produced in the first two essential phases (collection and sensemaking) is returned to the community and enters into collective discourse. Return may include formal reports and communications, and it may involve a wide or narrow distribution of collected stories. These informal storytellings may be more influential than the formal outputs, for better or worse, and they merit attention on their own terms.
